# Claim boundary checklist

## Supported after current evidence

- CAF-myeloid-high regions mark reproducible spatial cores in public PDAC spatial-transcriptomic cohorts.
- CAF-core enrichment is stronger than same-size spatially contiguous random tissue cores across the dominant primary and liver-metastasis contexts.
- IFN/MHC, immune-core, tumor-aggressive and TGF-beta/EMT gradients are not explained by direct marker overlap with the CAF-myeloid core definition.
- CAF-only and myeloid-only components make target-specific contributions; the combined anchor is balanced but not universally superior for every target program.
- Primary tumors and liver metastases show immune-coupled CAF-core architecture.
- A five-sample lymph-node metastasis subset suggests selective immune/IFN decoupling.
- Rank-4 NMF resolves reproducible CAF-core ecological axes in the 143-sample ecotype matrix.
- Matrix-integrin and SPP1-CD44/integrin are candidate axes for future perturbation.
- GSE274557 provides broad external Visium validation across non-LN metastatic contexts, but not lymph-node-specific validation.
- GSE274673 Xenium provides targeted-panel cell-resolution support for CAF-domain organization, not whole-transcriptome cell-state abundance.
- TCGA PAAD provides non-spatial bulk context and exploratory univariable survival context.
- H&E features provide an exploratory morphology bridge.

## Not supported

- Causal CAF-core organization of immune or tumor programs.
- Definitive lymph-node clinical subtype.
- A mathematically unique four-ecotype solution; rank 4 is an interpretable working resolution supported by stability and reconstruction.
- SPP1/TAM and myCAF/matrix as independent non-overlapping target modules, because they are core-component interpretation modules.
- Mature TLS biology without histologic/FDC/GC validation.
- Clinical-grade H&E prediction.
- TCGA spatial validation.
- Clinical validation, prognosis model or treatment-response prediction.
- Whole-transcriptome cell-state validation from the targeted Xenium panel.
- Single-cell-resolved spatial abundance from reference projection or NNLS.
